The main difference between the **BMS** and **BMD** series is that one is an **integrated solution** and the other is a **standalone component**.

Here is the breakdown of their key differences:

| Feature | BMS Series (Integrated Servo Gearmotor) | BMD Series (Standalone Servo Motor) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **What it is** | A complete, pre-assembled unit that combines a **servo motor and a precision planetary gearbox** into a single, compact housing. | A **standalone permanent magnet AC synchronous servo motor**. It is a single component that needs to be paired with a separate gearbox. |
| **Components** | Motor + Gearbox (Integrated) | Motor Only |
| **Output** | Provides **output torque** directly at the gearbox shaft. The output torque is the motor torque multiplied by the gear ratio. | Provides **motor torque** at its own shaft, which then needs to be transmitted to a gearbox. |
| **Design Philosophy** | **Integrated mechatronic solution**: Designed for revolutionary space-saving machine designs and optimized performance by leveraging the synergies between motor and gearbox technologies. | **Modular component**: Designed as a high-performance motor that offers flexibility to be paired with various gearboxes to create a customized solution. |
| **Benefits** | - **Saves space**: More compact than a separate motor and gearbox.<br>- **Higher performance**: Synergies between components lead to better overall performance.<br>- **Simplifies selection and assembly**: Comes as a single unit, reducing design time and potential compatibility issues. | - **High flexibility**: Can be combined with a wide range of gearboxes (like TQ, TQF, TR series) from Bonfiglioli or other manufacturers.<br>- **Application versatility**: Allows for a more customized solution if specific motor or gearbox characteristics are required. |
| **Key Specs** | Output torque: 8 to 800 Nm | Available in 6 frame sizes (65, 82, 102, 118, 145, 170) with a stall torque range of 0.85 to 45 Nm (up to 60 Nm with forced ventilation). |
